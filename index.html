Why this exists

The apps promised connection. They sold us loneliness.

AI and streaming turned culture into an algorithm — ranked, served, optimised for engagement and extraction, not for us. Queer life, built today on the backs of a lost generation of creatives and talents, will not be lost again — in person or in print. Minorities are the most exposed under a new, automated, biased AI infrastructure: spaces close, individuality and culture disappear, and the queer community gets treated as an audience of data to be sold, time and time again. Not anymore.

No trusted measure of queer connection — or a collated, proud queer reference for the businesses and practitioners that exist today — exists in Europe or the UK. If online presence and data can be deleted, so can we. Again.

So we’re building both: a living, ethical digital app, and a physical, hard-printed copy — for archive and perpetuity.

Who’s behind it

Built, created, imagined and driven by Joseph James.

A Welsh-born, European-raised and London-based creative director. These initiatives are not just from anywhere; they are a culmination of true lived experience and problems I have seen countless times: loneliness when moving to new cities, disconnection and isolation in home countries, and disconnection more than ever in a world of algorithms, AI, tech advances and social media that is only gripping us all tighter every day.

It also blends my creative practice and background in arts and cultural strategy over the last 12 years and community-initiative building, paired with work alongside large queer tech and advertising companies, having lived and assimilated to queer life in New York City, the US, Cardiff & London, UK, Germany, Greece and Sydney, Australia. It is my passion and life goal to ensure Queer connection, and the resilience of being a small business owner or practitioner, is measured, proven and protected, not optimised away from or forgotten by ever-advancing algorithmic monocultures. My four-phase initiatives and outcomes promise that no queer individual will be forgotten for trying to create their dream and offer something to the world.

Phase 1 establishes the UK framework of The Queer Connection Index, while the research, Collective and community-building are built with grassroots partners in Athens, in active partnership with The British Council Greece, and with current letters of support from multiple UK companies and a board member of the London Gay Men’s Chorus, Classical Pride and the world’s most-streamed a cappella group, The King’s Singers.

Questions

The honest answers.

Is this an investment? Do I get equity?

No. Your contribution is a gift that funds Phase 1 — you receive recognition and perks, not shares, and keeping the research body independent is exactly why we’re not raising equity now. The one exception we’re building in: Polari Circle supporters will be offered first option to take equity at Phase 3, if and when the timing is right, in accordance with UK and European ethics and strict, queer-backed, collectively governed models.

Can I give anonymously?

Yes, on any tier. No name, no public listing — just a clean contribution. Choose the anonymous option at checkout.

What if Phase 1 timelines move?

Research and partnerships shift. We report progress publicly and put your gift only toward the Phase 1 work described here.

Where does this benefit?

A UK-led, European-born reference: it routes back to the UK through the British Council Greece and proves the concept in Athens. Phase 1 is explicitly designed as a proof of concept for replication in Berlin, Amsterdam, Lisbon and other EU cultural capitals with active LGBTQ+ creative sectors.

What happens to The Only Pink Pages and Polari Atlas?

They’re the durable outputs — one printed, one digital — designed so the work survives outside any single platform.
